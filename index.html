About

Turning complex disease data into actionable insights

A dedicated epidemiologist with a strong academic foundation and extensive practical experience in transboundary epidemiology and data science. With a Ph.D. in Epidemiology, Nicolas has developed deep expertise in data analysis, disease modeling, geospatial analysis, and biostatistics.

His work bridges mathematical modeling and real-world policy, helping governments and organizations build rapid, evidence-based responses to disease outbreaks. An international profile spanning Colombia, Brazil, Spain, and the United States underscores his commitment to global animal health.

Research

Main Research Areas

01 / Research

Multispecies Disease Modeling

Builds stochastic and compartmental models for diseases such as foot-and-mouth disease, explicitly representing species-specific transmission and farm-level dynamics across large production landscapes.

02 / Research

Livestock Movement Networks

Uses network science to map between-farm animal movements, detect high-impact nodes, and estimate epidemic pathways in complex multi-species production systems.

03 / Research

Control Strategy Evaluation

Compares interventions — depopulation, vaccination, targeted movement restrictions — to support rapid, evidence-based outbreak response policies and contingency planning.

04 / Research

GIS & Spatial Analysis

Applies geospatial methods to identify disease clusters, characterize regional risk patterns, and support surveillance and preparedness planning across national livestock systems.
