Main Research

1. Multispecies Disease Modeling

Builds stochastic and compartmental models for diseases such as foot-and-mouth disease, explicitly representing species-specific transmission and farm-level dynamics.

2. Livestock Movement Networks

Uses network science to map between-farm animal movements, detect high-impact nodes, and estimate epidemic pathways in production systems.

3. Control Strategy Evaluation

Compares interventions such as depopulation, vaccination, and targeted movement restrictions to support rapid, evidence-based outbreak response policies.

4. GIS and Spatial Analysis

Applies geospatial methods to identify disease clusters, characterize regional risk patterns, and support surveillance and preparedness planning across livestock systems.
